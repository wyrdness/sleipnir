# Sources & References

## Ancient Primary Sources

### Poetic Edda (13th century, compiled from earlier oral tradition)

- *Grímnismál* (The Lay of Grímnir). Mentions Sleipnir as Odin's horse and best of steeds.
- *Baldrs draumar* (Baldr's Dreams). References Sleipnir in context of journey to Hel.
- *Sigrdrífumál* (The Lay of Sigrdrífa). Contains references to runes on Sleipnir's teeth.
- *Hyndluljóð* (The Lay of Hyndla). Mentions Sleipnir.

### Prose Edda by Snorri Sturluson (~1220 CE)

- *Gylfaginning* (The Beguiling of Gylfi). Chapters 41-42. Detailed account of Sleipnir's birth during the building of Asgard's wall. Chapter 49 describes Hermóðr's journey to Hel on Sleipnir.
- *Skáldskaparmál* (The Language of Poetry). Additional references to Sleipnir.

### Sagas

- *Völsunga saga* (13th century). References to Sleipnir and Odin.
- *Hervarar saga ok Heiðreks* (13th century). Mentions Sleipnir.
